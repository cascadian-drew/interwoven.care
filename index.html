About Us

Care that begins with relationship

Interwoven Family Care was founded by Bri in Tacoma, Washington, out of a simple conviction: children flourish when their whole family is supported. Rather than dropping care into a household like a service call, we weave into the life that's already there — your rhythms, your values, your way of doing things.

Every engagement starts with listening. Before we ever pick up a thread, we get to know your family's particular weave: what's working, what's strained, and where a steady hand would help most.

Our Philosophy

One thread at a time, the whole cloth holds

“Childcare is one thread in the larger weave of family support.”

Families aren't a collection of separate needs — they're a fabric, woven from relationships, routines, and care. When one thread is strained, the whole cloth feels it. And when support is woven in thoughtfully, everything holds together more easily.

That's why our care is adaptive by design. The support a family needs in a newborn season looks nothing like what it needs during a move, a new school year, or a hard stretch. We adjust as your family's weave changes — because it will.

FAQs

Questions families ask us

What areas do you serve?

We're based in Tacoma, Washington, and work with families in the surrounding area. Not sure if you're in range? Reach out — we'll figure it out together.

What does “adaptive” support actually mean?

It means the shape of our support follows the shape of your life. As your family's needs shift — new baby, new school, new season — what we do shifts with them, without you having to start over.

What ages do you work with?

Because we support the family as a whole, there's no strict cutoff. Tell us about your children and your household, and we'll talk about what fits.

How does scheduling work?

It starts with a conversation about what your family actually needs — regular weekly support, help through a transition, or something in between. We build the schedule from there.

What are your rates?

Rates depend on the shape of support your family needs. We'll talk through it openly in your first conversation — no surprises.
