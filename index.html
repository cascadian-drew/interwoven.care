About IFC

Support that sees the whole family

Interwoven Family Care was built by Brianna Waugh (Bri) after years of working directly with families and seeing proof of one simple truth: people do best when they are seen, understood, and supported as a whole.

Families flourish when they are supported as a whole. People flourish when they are understood.

Interwoven Family Care weaves into your life as it is: your rhythms, your values, your systems, your chaos, your calm. We trust you. We believe families should remain active participants in decisions that affect their lives. Your goals matter. Your values matter. Your voice matters.

Support works best when it is done with families, not to them or for them. Meaningful support begins with understanding.

Interwoven Family Care helps people build confidence in their own observations, decisions, and understanding of their families. We believe community care is self-care. Families become resources for one another, strengthening connection, belonging, and support throughout the community.

This work attracts all kinds of families who need individualized, practical support in their home and everyday life. We are for households that are overworked, overwhelmed, and then some.

Caregivers carry so much. Let Interwoven Family Care come alongside you and yours with practical, relationship-based support rooted in trust, respect, connection, and sustainability in your real life.

Offerings

Practical Support for You and Yours

Family Support

Relationship-based care shaped for your family's unique needs, strengths, and goals.

Support may include caregiver support, childcare, problem-solving, emotional support, and having a trusted sounding board as you navigate everyday life.

Breathing Room

Sometimes help is having another set of capable hands.

Practical support that helps reduce overwhelm, create capacity, and make space for your family to focus on what matters most.

Routines & Transitions

Bedtime. Mornings. Leaving the house. Sometimes the smallest parts of the day take the most energy.

Daily routines and transitions quietly shape the tone of an entire day. Support may include emotional regulation, skill-building, scaffolding independence, and practical strategies that help your daily life feel steadier and more manageable.

Advocacy Support

Navigating schools, providers, and systems while carrying the realities of family life is harder than it needs to be.

Advocacy includes meeting preparation, priority planning, and building confidence as you continue to advocate for yourself and your family.

Implementation & Resources

Turning ideas, resources, and recommendations from trusted professionals into practical next steps.

We will focus on solutions that fit your real life, capacity, and circumstances.

Environment Support

Support shaping spaces indoors and out so daily life functions with less friction.

Support may include environments that encourage independence, regulation, and routines that make sense for the people who live there.

Every family is different. Support is built around your needs, strengths, goals, and circumstances. If you don't see yourself reflected here, reach out anyway. Meaningful support begins with a conversation.

FAQs

Questions families ask us

What areas do you serve?

We're based in Tacoma, Washington, and work with families in the surrounding area. Not sure if you're in range? Reach out — we'll figure it out together.

Do you support neurodivergent or higher-needs kids?

Yes — it's the heart of our practice. Autistic kids, kids with ADHD, kids with big feelings or complex support needs: we meet each one exactly where they are and build trust at their pace. Tell us about your kid — we'd love to meet them.

What does “adaptive” support actually mean?

It means the shape of our support follows the shape of your life — built from scratch around your actual situation, not picked from a menu, and re-shaped as your needs change. A few hours a week for a single parent, help through a move or a new sibling, support shaped around one kid's particular needs: if it would genuinely help your family, it's on the table.

What ages do you work with?

Because we support the family as a whole, there's no strict cutoff. Tell us about your children and your household, and we'll talk about what fits.

How does scheduling work?

It starts with a conversation about what your family actually needs — regular weekly support, help through a transition, or something in between. We build the schedule from there.

What are your rates?

Rates depend on the shape of support your family needs. We'll talk through it openly in your first conversation — no surprises.
