What feels the hardest?

Every family brings a different mix of people, needs, schedules, capacity, and goals. These are not packages because all families are unique.

The first conversation is not a test. You do not need a polished explanation, perfect plan, or even to know which exact service you might want. Start with what feels hard. We'll talk through support that fits.

A quick note before the examples: these example families are fictional, but the kinds of needs are real. They are here to help you picture what support can look like and what an approximate price point might be.

IFC is not therapy, clinical treatment, legal representation, special education representation, formal case management, medical care, or crisis support.

Depending on the family, care may include any of these or more:

Not every family needs all of this.

  • Childcare or child-related care
  • Relief and respite care
  • Routines and transition help
  • Advocacy preparation
  • Caregiver/parent support and breathing room
  • Resource navigation
  • Implementation & Resources
  • Environment support
